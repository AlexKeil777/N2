What do we do?

Build robust VHP decontamination H₂O₂ removal

The project objective is to address VHP decontamination challenges through two complementary angles: (1) shift from continuous tunnel models to a modular approach, and (2) optimize the aeration phase, often the bottleneck, to accelerate peroxide desorption.

Focus 1 — Rethinking the process architecture

We aim to move away from large continuous tunnel sections toward a modular, decoupled concept that can maintain sterility assurance while improving operational robustness and handling interruptions more gracefully.

What this enables

  • Independent modules that decouple cycles and reduce reliance on interconnected containment flows.
  • Improved reliability and scalability by design (configure throughput by adding/removing modules).

Focus 2 — Improving aeration (speed + depth)

We focus on aerodynamic and thermodynamic optimization of aeration, often the bottleneck in VHP-based cycles.

What we optimize

  • Airflow, temperature, and moisture control to accelerate peroxide desorption.
  • Chamber design to achieve optimal aeration, ensuring safety and repeatability.

Where this is heading

The outcome we’re aiming for is a modular VHP approach that improves scalability and reliability, paired with a faster aeration method to reach low residual targets efficiently.
