Our mission

Deliver robust VHP decontamination and enable faster, deeper H₂O₂ removal

Our mission is built on a two-part strategy: (1) redesign the overall process architecture for reliability and scalability, and (2) improve aeration to achieve faster cycles and lower residuals — especially important for sensitive products and isolator-based manufacturing.

Part 1 — Rethinking the process architecture (batch vs. continuous)

We propose shifting from a continuous tunnel model to a batch process concept, aiming to keep output performance while improving reliability, flexibility, and scalability.

Why batch?

  • Lower R&D effort and construction complexity by leveraging tighter, closed stations rather than flow-through sections.
  • Robust process decoupling: independent time-bound cycles remain consistent even during stops; if one station fails, others keep running.
  • No over-exposure risk on downstream stops (treatment cycle completes without unintended extension).
  • Flexible recipes: stations enable customizable cycles compared to tunnel designs tightly linked to a single process.
  • Bidirectional material flow without needing emptying or format part changes typical of one-direction tunnel flow.
  • Modular design & scalability: configurations can match throughput needs without tunnel layout constraints.
  • Market differentiation through clear distinction from proprietary tunnel technologies.

Part 2 — Exploiting H₂O₂ evaporation dynamics (faster & deeper aeration)

Current aeration processes show room for improvement in both speed and depth of residual removal, particularly for highly H₂O₂-sensitive products and isolator environments with limited human access.

Approach

Improve removal performance through airflow and dew-point management, leveraging evaporation dynamics to reduce real ppm/ppb residuals in practical time.

Expected value

  • Faster cycles to 1 ppm for standard applications (early tests suggest significant reductions).
  • Lower residuals (e.g., below 100 ppb) for highly sensitive products where this is currently difficult.
  • Manufacturing scheduling benefits: shorter set-up time, easier troubleshooting and faster restart after interventions.
  • System-level impact: shorter cycles can reduce the number of batch stations needed for the same tubs/min throughput.
  • Applicability: suitable for both new isolators and retrofits.

Next steps

We outline development and industrialization in partnership with an established manufacturer, with support on design and patent rights; plus R&D toward technique scale-up and continued aeration sequence improvement.
